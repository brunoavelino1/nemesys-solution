Any Questions? Answered

To get your 30-day free-trial license, provide the MAC address of the machine where Nemesys CFD will be installed either by email or Whatsapp. We will schedule a guided setup to help you unlock its full potential. During the trial period, you can run an unlimited number of simulations.

To get started, please provide your CFD test requirements, a Mesh Geometry File (.geo or .su2), and details about the simulation. This includes the physics and models involved (such as fluid or thermal interactions), thermal properties of the materials, and the boundary and initial conditions.

Once we receive this information, we will handle pre-processing, solver setup, and simulation preparation, run the Nemesys solver, and export the results in .vtk or .vtu formats.

In the future we’ll be able to convert .msh files directly

Nemesys CFD requires an Nvidia GPU with Compute Capability 8.0 or higher. You can verify compatibility here. For the best performance, we recommend a GPU with at least 16GB of VRAM.

To integrate Nemesys, generate a .su2 mesh using your preferred software (e.g. Gmsh) and configure YAML files for solver settings, conditions, output, and material properties. Run the Nemesys solver to process your simulation, then locate the results in the ../ans/ folder (.vtu/.vtk) and analyze them using ParaView or Visit.
